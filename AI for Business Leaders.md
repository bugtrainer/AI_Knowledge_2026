# 🏢 AI for Business Leaders — Executive Briefing

## Questions Every Leader Should Be Able to Answer

### Operations & Adoption
1. Where are we using AI today, and where are we not — and why?
2. Are there workflows in our organisation that [[AI Agent]]s could automate?
3. Have we evaluated [[OpenClaw]] or [[NemoClaw (NVIDIA)|NemoClaw]] for internal agent deployments?
4. Are non-technical staff building tools with [[Lovable]] or [[Bolt.new (StackBlitz)|Bolt]]? What governance do we need?

### Data & Trust
5. Do we have a policy on what data employees can share with AI systems?
6. How are we managing [[Hallucination]] risk in any AI outputs we rely on?
7. Are our teams using [[Perplexity AI]] and [[NotebookLM]] for research?

### Strategy & Risk
8. What is our position on [[AI Sovereignty]] — are we dependent on a single provider?
9. How are we thinking about the impact of AI on our workforce over the next three years?
10. Are we investing in [[Prompt Engineering]] and AI literacy across our teams?

### Regulatory
11. Are we compliant with the [[EU AI Act]]'s upcoming August 2026 high-risk AI deadlines?
12. What is our strategy for AI-generated content authentication ([[C2PA]])?

### Innovation
13. Have we assessed the impact of [[Reasoning Model]]s on our analytical workflows?
14. Are we evaluating AI for science opportunities — drug discovery, materials, or simulation?

---

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The organisations that thrive in 2026 are not necessarily the ones with the biggest AI budgets — they're the ones asking the right questions and deploying AI with clear governance.
