# 🔙 Backpropagation

## Definition
**Backpropagation** is the fundamental algorithm that allows [[Neural Network]]s to learn. It works by calculating the error of the model's prediction, then propagating that error **backwards** through the network to adjust the weights — making the model slightly better with each iteration.

## Real-World Analogy
Imagine a student takes a test and gets an answer wrong. The teacher doesn't just say "wrong" — they trace back through the student's reasoning to find exactly where the mistake happened, and correct that specific step. Backpropagation does this mathematically.

## Why It Matters
- Pioneered by [[Geoffrey Hinton]] in the 1980s — considered by many as the foundational insight that made modern AI possible
- Without backpropagation, we couldn't train deep neural networks
- It's how every [[LLM]], image model, and AI system learns

## Key Relationships
- Pioneered by: [[Geoffrey Hinton]]
- Trains: [[Neural Network]], [[Deep Learning]]
- Uses: Gradient descent, calculus
