## Definition
**C2PA** (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity) is a technical standard for embedding **invisible metadata** into digital content (images, video, audio) that proves whether it was created by a human or AI — and tracks its editing history.

## Why It Matters in 2026
- Increasingly required by streaming platforms and social media
- Key defence against deepfakes and AI-generated misinformation
- The [[EU AI Act]] pushes for content authentication requirements
- Major AI image/video tools (Midjourney, DALL-E, Adobe Firefly) now embed C2PA metadata

## Key Relationships
- Addresses: Deepfakes, AI-generated misinformation
- Required by: [[EU AI Act]]
- Used by: [[Adobe Firefly 3]], [[Midjourney v7]]
