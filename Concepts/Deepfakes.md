# 🎭 Deepfakes

## Definition
AI-generated synthetic media — fake videos, images, or audio that convincingly depict real people doing or saying things they never did. Originally created using [[GAN]]s, now increasingly made with [[Diffusion Model]]s and voice cloning tools like [[ElevenLabs]]. A major trust and security challenge.

## How It Works (Simplified)
1. AI models are trained on images, video, or voice samples of a target person
2. The model learns to generate new, realistic content of that person
3. Face-swapping, lip-syncing, and voice cloning can produce convincing fakes
4. Modern tools make deepfakes creatable in minutes by non-experts

## Why It Matters in 2026
- Deepfake fraud has caused billions of dollars in losses (fake CEO voice calls authorising transfers)
- Election interference via deepfake political videos is a growing concern
- [[C2PA]] content authentication standards were created specifically to combat deepfakes
- Detection is an arms race — detectors improve, but so do generation techniques

> [!tip] Key Fact
> In 2024, a finance worker in Hong Kong was tricked into transferring $25M after a deepfake video call that convincingly impersonated multiple company executives.
