## Definition
The **EU AI Act** is the world's first comprehensive AI regulation. It establishes a **risk-based framework** that classifies AI systems by their potential for harm and imposes corresponding requirements.

## Timeline
| Date | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Feb 2025 | Prohibited AI practices enforced (social scoring, facial recognition mass surveillance) |
| Aug 2025 | Transparency rules for general-purpose AI |
| Aug 2026 | High-risk AI system rules take effect |
| Aug 2027 | Full enforcement |

## Penalties
- Up to **€35 million** or **7% of global annual turnover** (whichever is higher)
- More severe than GDPR fines

## Why It Matters in 2026
- Sets the global standard — other countries are modelling their AI laws on it
- Companies like [[Mistral AI]] champion "European AI sovereignty" in response
- Every enterprise deploying AI in Europe must assess compliance
- [[Guardrails]] and [[Interpretability]] are partially driven by regulatory requirements
