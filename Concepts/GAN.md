# 🎭 GAN

## Definition
A **Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)** consists of two neural networks competing against each other: a **Generator** (creates fake content) and a **Discriminator** (tries to detect fakes). Through this adversarial training, the generator becomes increasingly good at producing realistic outputs.

## Real-World Analogy
A counterfeiter (generator) tries to create fake paintings. An art expert (discriminator) tries to spot the fakes. Over time, the counterfeiter gets so good that even the expert can't tell the difference.

## Why It Matters
- Invented by **Ian Goodfellow** in 2014 — one of the most creative ideas in AI
- Was the dominant image generation approach before [[Diffusion Model]]s took over (~2022)
- Still used for style transfer, super-resolution, and data augmentation

## Key Relationships
- Type of: [[Neural Network]], [[Generative AI]]
- Replaced by: [[Diffusion Model]] for image generation
- Related: [[Deep Learning]]
