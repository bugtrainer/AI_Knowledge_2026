# 🦙 Llama

## Definition
[[Meta AI]]'s family of open-source large language models. The Llama series — Llama 1 (2023), Llama 2, Llama 3, Llama 3.1 405B, and Llama 4 — became the most important open-source AI models, spawning 10,000+ derivative models and challenging the dominance of closed-source labs like [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]].

## How It Works (Simplified)
1. Meta trains Llama models at massive scale using internal GPU clusters
2. Trained model weights are released publicly under permissive licenses (originally Meta license, then Community License)
3. Anyone can download, run locally ([[Ollama]]), fine-tune ([[LoRA]], [[QLoRA]]), and build products on Llama
4. Community creates thousands of specialised derivative models on [[Hugging Face]]

## Why It Matters in 2026
- Llama 2's release (July 2023) was AI's "Linux moment" — it proved open-source could compete with proprietary models
- Llama 3.1 405B (2024) was the first open-source model to genuinely compete with GPT-4
- Llama 4's small models enable AI on phones, laptops, and edge devices
- [[Mark Zuckerberg]]'s strategic bet to commoditise foundation models reshaped the entire industry

> [!tip] Key Fact
> Llama 2 spawned 10,000+ derivative models within 3 months of release — more than any open-source AI project in history.
