## Definition
**Model Collapse** is a phenomenon where AI models trained recursively on AI-generated ([[Synthetic Data]]) content degrade in quality over time — losing diversity, accuracy, and coherence.

## Real-World Analogy
It's like making a photocopy of a photocopy of a photocopy — each generation loses quality. When AI models train on their own outputs (which increasingly fill the internet), the same degradation occurs.

## Why It Matters in 2026
- The internet is increasingly flooded with AI-generated text
- Future models trained on this data risk lower quality
- Labs are investing heavily in **curating high-quality human data**
- [[Data-Centric AI]] emerged partly as a response to this risk

## Key Relationships
- Caused by: [[Synthetic Data]] (recursive training)
- Mitigated by: [[Data-Centric AI]], human data curation
- Related: [[Pre-Training]], [[AI Safety]]
