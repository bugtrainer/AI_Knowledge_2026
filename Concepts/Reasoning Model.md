## Definition
A **Reasoning Model** is a new class of AI that dedicates additional compute time to "think" step-by-step **before** generating an answer. Unlike standard LLMs that produce output immediately, reasoning models use internal [[Chain-of-Thought]] to backtrack, self-correct, and handle complex logic, math, science, and coding problems.

## How It Works
```
Standard LLM: Prompt → Immediate Answer (fast, sometimes wrong)
Reasoning Model: Prompt → Internal Thinking → Self-Correction → Verified Answer (slower, much more accurate)
```

## Real-World Analogy
A standard LLM is like answering a math question off the top of your head. A reasoning model is like taking out a piece of paper, working through the problem step by step, checking your work, and THEN giving the final answer.

## Why It Matters in 2026
Reasoning models represent a **paradigm shift** in AI capabilities:
- **OpenAI o3/o3-pro**: The flagship reasoning series — dramatically better at math, science, and code
- **OpenAI o4-mini**: Smaller, faster reasoning model for cost-sensitive applications
- **Mistral Magistral**: Open-weight reasoning model from Europe
- **DeepSeek-R1**: Open-source reasoning model at a fraction of the cost
- **Gemini 3.1 Deep Think**: Google's reasoning variant of Gemini
