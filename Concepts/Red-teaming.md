# 🔴 Red-teaming

## Definition
**Red-teaming** is the practice of adversarially probing an AI model to find vulnerabilities, harmful behaviours, and failure modes **before** deployment. Red-teamers try to make the model behave badly — so the developers can fix it.

## Why It Matters
- Standard practice at every major AI lab before model release
- Finds [[Prompt Injection]] vulnerabilities, [[Jailbreak]] techniques, and bias
- The [[EU AI Act]] effectively requires red-teaming for high-risk AI
- External red teams (e.g., from universities, security firms) provide independent assessment

## Key Relationships
- Tests for: [[Prompt Injection]], [[Jailbreak]], [[Hallucination]]
- Part of: [[AI Safety]], [[AI Alignment]]
- Required by: [[EU AI Act]]
- Fixed by: [[Guardrails]], [[Constitutional AI]]
