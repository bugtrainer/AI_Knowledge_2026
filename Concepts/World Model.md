## Definition
A **World Model** is an AI system that builds an internal representation of **physical reality** — enabling it to simulate outcomes, plan actions, and understand the 3D world. Unlike [[LLM]]s that understand text, world models understand **physics, space, and causality**.

## Real-World Analogy
An LLM is like someone who has read every book about driving but has never sat in a car. A world model is like someone who has an internal mental simulation of how cars, roads, gravity, and physics work — they can predict what happens if you turn the wheel without needing to read about it.

## Why It Matters in 2026
World models are seen as the **next frontier beyond LLMs** — and potentially the path to [[AGI]]:
- **[[Yann LeCun]]** (AMI Labs) raised $1.03B arguing LLMs alone will NEVER achieve AGI
- **[[Fei-Fei Li]]** (World Labs) launched **Marble** — the first commercial world model product
- **[[Google DeepMind]]** is developing **Genie 3** — real-time interactive world generation
- **[[NVIDIA Cosmos]]** trained on 20M hours of video for robotics training data
