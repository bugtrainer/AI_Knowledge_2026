# 0️⃣ Zero-Shot

## Definition
Asking a model to perform a task with **no examples** in the prompt — relying purely on pre-trained knowledge. Works surprisingly well for modern large models. Zero-shot capability increases dramatically with model scale, representing one of the most remarkable emergent abilities of [[LLM]]s.

## How It Works (Simplified)
1. You give the model a task description with no examples: "Translate this to French: Hello"
2. The model draws on patterns learned during [[Pre-Training]] to complete the task
3. No special formatting or demonstrations needed — just a clear instruction
4. Compare with [[Few-Shot]]: providing 2-5 examples before the actual task

## Why It Matters in 2026
- GPT-4 passes the Bar exam **zero-shot**, scoring in the 90th percentile
- Zero-shot capability is one of the strongest arguments that LLMs are genuine reasoners, not just pattern matchers
- It's the basis of all everyday AI assistant usage — users rarely provide examples before asking questions

> [!tip] Key Fact
> GPT-3 (2020) showed the first dramatic zero-shot capabilities. GPT-4 extended this to expert-level performance on bar exams, medical licensing, and advanced math — all without seeing a single example.
