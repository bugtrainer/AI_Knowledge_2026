# Core Concepts Overview

4. The Most Important AI Concepts in 2026

### 4.1 Large Language Models (LLMs)
The engine behind most modern AI tools. Trained on vast amounts of text to predict and generate language. Powers ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and virtually every AI assistant.

**Analogy:** Think of an LLM as an extraordinarily well-read assistant. It has absorbed more text than any human could read in a lifetime — but it generates new language rather than retrieving stored answers. This is why it can sometimes confidently produce incorrect information (hallucination).

### 4.2 AI Agents
The defining development of 2025-2026. An AI agent does not just answer a question — it takes a sequence of actions to complete a goal, using tools, browsing the web, writing code, sending emails, and making decisions along the way.

**Analogy:** A traditional AI assistant is like a knowledgeable colleague you ask questions. An AI agent is like giving that colleague a computer, access to all your systems, and asking them to run an entire project end-to-end — while you go do something else.

### 4.3 Reasoning Models
A new class of AI models (OpenAI's o-series, Mistral's Magistral, DeepSeek-R1) that dedicate additional compute time to "think" step-by-step before generating an answer. They use internal chain-of-thought to backtrack, self-correct, and handle complex logic, math, science, and coding problems.

### 4.4 Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
A technique that gives an AI access to your specific knowledge base — company documents, databases, manuals — at the time of answering. It retrieves relevant information first, then generates an answer grounded in that information. Dramatically reduces hallucination and makes AI answers traceable to sources.

### 4.5 World Models
The next frontier beyond LLMs. A world model builds an internal representation of physical reality — enabling the AI to simulate outcomes, plan actions, and understand the 3D world, not just text. Critical for robotics, autonomous vehicles, and embodied AI.

**Who Is Racing to Build This in 2026:** Yann LeCun (AMI Labs, $1.03B raised), Fei-Fei Li (World Labs, Marble product launched), and Google DeepMind (Genie 3). This is seen as a potential path to more general AI beyond LLMs.

### 4.6 Hallucination
When an AI generates information that sounds plausible but is factually incorrect. Remains the most important limitation for enterprise deployments. Mitigation: RAG, human review, citation requirements, grounding.

### 4.7 Multimodal AI
AI that processes and generates across multiple types of content: text, images, audio, video, and data. All leading 2026 models (GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1, Claude Opus 4.6, Muse Spark) are multimodal.

### 4.8 Vibe Coding
Using AI to write most or all of the code in a software project — by describing what you want in plain English. Now mainstream in 2026. AI writes roughly 30% of Microsoft's code and over 25% of Google's. Non-engineers are building functional apps with tools like Lovable and Replit in hours.

### 4.9 Embodied AI & Physical AI
AI systems that interact with the physical world through robotic bodies. The convergence of LLMs, computer vision, and world models is enabling humanoid robots (Figure, Tesla Optimus) and autonomous vehicles (Waymo) to operate in unpredictable real-world environments using end-to-end neural networks.

### 4.10 AI for Science
AI is accelerating scientific discovery at unprecedented pace. AlphaFold 3 has become foundational for drug discovery, protein folding, and molecular interaction prediction. The first "fully AI-designed" drug molecules are entering clinical trials. AI is also transforming materials science, climate modelling, and genomics.
