# 👴 Geoffrey Hinton
**"Godfather of Deep Learning"** — Turing Award 2018

## Role & Significance
Pioneered the algorithms that make modern AI possible. His work on [[Backpropagation]] (1986), Boltzmann machines, dropout, and deep neural networks laid the mathematical foundation for the entire AI era. Won the Turing Award (2018) alongside [[Yann LeCun]] and [[Yoshua Bengio]]. Left Google in 2023 to speak freely about AI existential risks.

## Key Contributions
- **Backpropagation** (1986) — Co-authored the paper that made [[Neural Network]]s trainable
- **Boltzmann Machines** — Pioneered probabilistic generative networks
- **ImageNet / AlexNet** (2012) — His student [[Ilya Sutskever]] + Alex Krizhevsky won ImageNet, launching the [[Deep Learning]] revolution
- **Dropout** — Regularisation technique now standard in all neural network training
- **Capsule Networks** — Proposed alternative to CNNs (ongoing research)

## In 2026
Continues advocacy for AI safety regulation. Frequently speaks at conferences about existential risks from advanced AI. Nobel Prize in Physics (2024) for contributions to machine learning.

> [!quote] Notable Quote
> "I'm now afraid of what I've built. The idea that this stuff could get smarter than us and decide to take over — I think the probability of that is not negligible."
