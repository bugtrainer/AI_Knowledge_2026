# 🏢 Sam Altman
**CEO, OpenAI** (since 2019)

## Role & Significance
The most prominent public face of AI globally. Under his leadership, OpenAI launched ChatGPT (Nov 2022), GPT-4, DALL-E, Sora, and o1 — triggering the generative AI revolution. Previously served as President of Y Combinator. His vision for [[AGI]] as OpenAI's primary goal drives the company's strategy.

## Key Contributions
- Led launch of **ChatGPT** (Nov 2022) — fastest-growing consumer app in history (100M users in 2 months)
- Oversaw development of **GPT-4** (2023) — the first model to pass the Bar exam, CPA exam, and medical licensing exams
- Negotiated the **$13B Microsoft partnership** — the largest AI investment in history
- Championed the push toward AGI as a concrete engineering goal, not just a research aspiration

## In 2026
Attended India AI Summit (Feb 2026) alongside global leaders. Under scrutiny after a New Yorker investigation. Publicly frustrated that AI adoption faces more resistance than expected. OpenAI introduced ads for free ChatGPT users — sparking controversy.

> [!quote] Notable Quote
> "AI will be the most transformative technology humanity has ever built — more consequential than fire, electricity, or the internet."

## The Board Crisis (Nov 2023)
In the most dramatic corporate episode in AI history, Altman was fired by OpenAI's board on Nov 17, 2023. Within 5 days, 95% of employees threatened to quit, Microsoft offered to hire the entire team, and Altman was reinstated as CEO with a restructured board.
