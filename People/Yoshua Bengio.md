# 🇨🇦 Yoshua Bengio
**Scientific Director, MILA** — Turing Award 2018

## Role & Significance
Third member of the "Deep Learning Trinity" alongside [[Geoffrey Hinton]] and [[Yann LeCun]]. Pioneered attention mechanisms, generative models, and neural machine translation. Now a prominent voice advocating for strict AI regulation and existential risk mitigation. Leads the world's largest academic AI lab (MILA) in Montreal.

## Key Contributions
- **Attention Mechanism** (2014) — Co-created the attention concept that evolved into [[Transformer]]s
- **Neural Machine Translation** — Pioneered sequence-to-sequence models for translation
- **Word Embeddings** — Foundational work on representing words as dense vectors ([[Embedding]])
- **GFlowNet** — Recent research on generative flow networks for discrete optimisation
- **AI Governance** — Co-authored open letters calling for AI moratoriums and regulation

## In 2026
Active advocate for international AI governance. Testified before multiple governments on AI risks. Co-signed open letters calling for pause on frontier model development. Leads MILA's research on responsible AI.

> [!quote] Notable Quote
> "We're creating entities that are more powerful than us. It would be insane not to put serious effort into making sure this goes well."
